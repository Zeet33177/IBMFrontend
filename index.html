Sunlight Zone

The uppermost layer of the ocean, where sunlight penetrates and supports a vibrant ecosystem. But this beautiful place faces threat from Overfishing & Pollution which disrupts the life of aquatic animals in many ways

The sunlight zone's diverse and vibrant ecosystem is vital for the health of the ocean and supports a significant portion of the world's fisheries​ .

The sunlight zone, also known as the epipelagic or euphotic zone, extends from the ocean's surface down to about 200 meters (660 feet).

Major Threats

Overfishing
Plastic Pollution
Oil Spills

Twilight Zone

The twilight zone can be found 200 to 1,000 meters below the ocean surface, at the point where the sun's rays can no longer reach. But , it's full of life including fish, crustaceans, jellyfish, squids and worms. Occasionally, there are bursts of bioluminescence , when living creatures give off their natural glows.

Why It needs your support ?
It make up more than 90% of all fish in the sea, according to the Blue Marine Foundation. Since some countries like Norway and Japan already started large scale removal of these fishes it is crucial to take fast action.
